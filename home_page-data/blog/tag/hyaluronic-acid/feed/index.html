]]> It is not uncommon to hear people talking about the ingredients contained in a beauty or skincare product. In fact, it is the basis with which most people purchase their products. However, with the like of hyaluronic acid (HA) hitting the shelves more recently with lots of skincare products, it has left us wondering whether or not to see it as the hope or it is just a heaped hype.

So, what exactly is HA? Does it offer any major benefits and are there any effective ways it can be applied than the normal application?

Explaining hyaluronic acid!

Actually, hyaluronic acid is a humectant. A kind of substance capable of retaining moisture and coercing it with more weight in liquid. Naturally, this substance can be found in every part of the human body. Actually, the kind of HA utilizes dome sort of bacteria to make beauty and skincare products by sourcing it via a process called bio-fermentation in the laboratory.

However, as we continue to grow older, these substances in our body, including hyaluronic acid continues to decrease, thereby causing our skin to lose plumpness, hydration, and adequate volume.

How much quality does Hyaluronic Acid produce tends to give our body?

From the look of things, most of these HA-induced products claim to increase the quality value of the skin. How true is this claim?

Well, we’ll have to take a deeper look into the type of HA available in the product. Usually, HA isn’t usually of the same molecular sizes. While some are broken down, some are excessively massive in size. Meanwhile, the latter can not penetrate into the skin easily despite being able to bind water and hydrate the body. So when such HA-product is applied to the skin, it had little or no effects on it. Only the topmost layer of the skin is overly influenced. However, the same cannot be said for products with a smaller quantity of molecules. These one’s plunge through and deeper into the skin with ease.

In some embodiments, Hyaluronic Acids serves as dermal fillers, which are usually composed of HA in an injectable gel form. HA fillers include volume by practically overlaying their placement area and drawing in water to improve the filling effect. Once it is perfectly done, manufacturers can make use of HA fillers to make products that influence a lot of ornamental concerns, such as cheeks lifting, skin softening, augmenting sunken, dark, undereye circles, hydrating and enriching the lips, as well as stimulating the hands and earlobes.

Is Hyaluronic worthy of the hype or is it just a sham?

So, is it safe to conclude that HA is the answer to our different cosmetic problems or is it just a hype from a selected public to sell products? To answer this, we must first establish the fact that topical HA is not as effective or strong as injectable HA filler which is capable of refurbishing lost skin quality. Although some topical HA products are ignorantly paraded as topical “fillers.” Notwithstanding, Topical HA is a terrific moisturizer. Yet, on the off chance that the major goal is to improve volume loss and skin autonomy that naturally comes with aging and dryness. Injectable HA would be a better choice and a more effective method than topical HA.

Don’t get this wrong, Topical HA has got lots of positive attributes. It tolerates quite well and does not usually cause allergic reactions which might result in skin irritation. More importantly, it is a good choice for pregnant and lactating mothers’ skin.

However, for individuals with dry skin, or those with dewier, moisturized skin feel cravings during the cooler periods, a moisturizer or serum-containing HA is a great choice to utilize. But then, you have to be constantly reminded that topical HA will only hydrate the surface level of your skin and not penetrate deeper within it.

Final verdict

There is a surrounding doubt about hyaluronic acid being the latest cosmetic ingredient trends. Virtually every skincare product you see available in the market today contains it. From anti-aging creams, serums, acne prevention washes, to mask, sunscreen and other products, you’ll definitely find them there.

However, just as it is with other accepted skincare ingredients, HA is only valuable when it is used rightly. Should you be at taking off the visibility of facial wrinkles or to moisturize your skin, a cosmetic product containing hyaluronic acid would be a good choice.

Meanwhile, the same cannot be said for those looking to prevent acne or patches of skin hyperpigmentation. Rather, it would be better you take a look at another direction. Else, you’d be wasting your resources on what isn’t going to work for you. While hyaluronic acid works well to clear off anti-aging wrinkles and dehydration, it cannot solve every skincare problem. So, make your choice wisely.
