]]> What is a reaction paper? Do you believe that many scholars find this term difficult to define and yes, why not! Most scholars only come across this paper a few times in their academic pursuits.

A reaction paper isn’t too technical, it’s still the same with the English word to “react”. You do know that for every action taken there is always a reaction. This is exactly what the examiner wants you to do when he gives you a literary story, he simply wants you to react and give your personal suggestions on the subject.

So in simple terms, a reaction paper is explained as the literary response towards a story. Before you begin, you’ll have to, analyze the book and get all the necessary information you need before giving an opinion on the matter. So the key thing when writing a reaction paper is “giving a suggestion on the matter.” All you have to do is to have followers, who will support your suggestion.

There’re two common basic fundamentals when writing a reaction paper, in which it comprises of two audiences, and they’re the readers and the referees.

The readers are those who read your work and the referee are those who decide what to post. It is also common to find your work as a reference by other writers who are working on the same subject. Check out these tips here, you might find it very helpful

Steps to writing a research paper

Before writing on any subject, and if you’re done reading the book. The first thing to ask yourself is “what is my take on this subject, how do I feel after reading this book?” you definitely have to know what the writer was trying to pass across.

Your presentation also matters because this can give you a high score when graded by your examiner. Here is a guide that would be very helpful for you when organizing a reaction paper:

Article Review Writing: The Preparation and Process Involved

  • Context: This deals with giving a brief background of the story. This helps to introduce the subject to the reader and guides the reader throughout the work.
  • Purpose: The first question on every reader’s mind would be “why review this work?” with such questions, you need to make them interested in reading your work by creating suspense.
  • Thesis: Include a thesis statement when presenting or introducing your work. A thesis generally includes a summary of the work.
  • Specific details of the book: This deals with giving basic details of the book, like for example; the author, and what the book is all about.

Outline of a Reaction Paper

To have superb content for the book you are analyzing, you have to follow these processes:

  1. Read or watch the movie: All you have to do here is to jot down the main points while watching or reading the book. For a book, you’ll have to read two times to become more acquainted with the theme of the book.
  2. Evaluation: Read the question of your examiner and make sure you’re following. Perhaps you may be asked to react to a part of the story, this, therefore, means you have to center your suggestions to that particular section. The second question by your examiner may be to give a detailed explanation of the opinion of the writer. To answer such a question, you’ll have to center on the writer’s thoughts as relating to his or her book.

Reaction paper format

  1. Introduction: Make sure the introduction captures the interest of the reader. In this part, you’ll have to describe the writer, the subject matter of the story and the general idea. A good introduction should have two to four sentences. And immediately after this, you write the thesis statement, which must also be short and precise.
  2. Body: This is where all the ideas as gotten from the book are written. In each paragraph, you should begin each one with a topic sentence. The topic sentence should deal mainly with passing across the information and also include the appropriate authorities on a subject or source to back up your suggestions.
  3. Conclusion: In this part of the paper, you should link your thesis statement with all the summary of ideas in each paragraph. A good way to end the reaction paper is by hitting the main points when making a clarification.
  4. Citations: You need to give credit to either the writer or any other source. This, therefore, includes, citing the sources you may have used in your reaction paper.
