Writting Errands's ideal scenario is a platform free of conflicts between students and writers. However, disputes can happen if a student requests a refund on their question. Our moderation team follows a set of clear guidelines for handling disputes when they occur. This ensures that all refund requests are handled fairly, with Writting Errands acting as an impartial mediator. Below is a table that outlines the most common reasons for a student requesting a refund on their question, and how Writting Errands will resolve the matter.

Reason for Refund RequestProcessResolution
PlagiarismWe check the students evidence, original question requirements, and we run our own plagiarism check.1. If our plagiarism check is more than 15%, we side with the student.
2. If our plagiarism check is less than 15% but the evidence is solid, we side with the student.
3. If our plagiarism check is less than 15% and evidence is neither present not solid, we side with the writer.
Bad GrammarWe check the tutoring session and final answer and validate whether the students claim is correct.1. If the writer exhibits bad grammar and we are able to identify multiple instances of grammar errors, we side with the student.
2. If the writer exhibits solid grammar, we side with the writer.
3. We encourage writers be mindful of their grammar and spelling when communicating with the student and helping them with their question.
OverdueWe check whether the writer delivered the answer late or didnt deliver it at all.1. If the writer is late without the student giving additional time, we side with the student.

2. If the writer did not deliver the answer, we side with the student.
3. If the writer is late, but within the additional time frame given by the student, we side with the writer.
4. If the writer delivered the answer within the time limit, we side with the writer.
Failure to Meet RequirementsWe check the students requirements and instructions, and the submitted evidence.1. If the writer does not meet 100% of the requirements, we side with the student.

2. If the writer promises additional explanations, but leaves the student waiting, we side with the student.
3. If evidence is not solid or specific enough and the answer seems to meet the requirements, we side with the writer.
4. If the writer has not agreed to answering additional questions from the student, we side with the writer.
CancellationWe check our system and the question information.1. If the writer complies with the posted instructions, we side with the writer.
2. If the writer agrees to the cancellation of the question, we refund and hide the writers review.
3. If the writer cancels the question incorrectly, we side with the student.
